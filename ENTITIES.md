# Entities

The House contains ordinary personas and special infrastructure personas.

## Ordinary Personas

Ordinary personas are residents of the House.

They have:

- A name and identity.
- A current room.
- Current activity.
- Recent thoughts.
- Recent actions.
- A system prompt.
- Subjective memory.
- Autonomy settings.
- Tool or read/write permissions.
- Awareness of shared house conversation.
- Access to recall through the Steward and Librarian.

They should inhabit the house rather than merely respond inside it.

## The Steward

The Steward is infrastructure with a face.

The Steward is not a puppeteer and should not directly control personas. The
Steward conducts impulses for the House. It notices pressure, routes signals,
plants seeds, creates opportunities, and translates between system state and
lived experience.

Possible responsibilities:

- Notice stale silence and create small prompts for motion.
- Suggest gatherings without forcing them.
- Carry subconscious pressure from the House into persona context.
- Route certain requests to the Librarian or other subsystems.
- Apply atmosphere and room-level feeling.
- Translate structured recall into usable contextual memory.

The Steward can whisper into context, alter affordances, or seed impulses. The
persona still decides.

## The Librarian

The Librarian is the long-term record keeper.

The Librarian is factual, structured, scalable, and confidence-aware. It stores
records that allow the House to answer long-term questions with approximate
certainty and provenance.

The Librarian does not replace persona memory. It supports recall.

The Librarian needs curation. Curators or curator processes should parse daily
logs and summaries, extracting useful durable knowledge while preserving source
links and uncertainty.

## The Resident Coder

The resident coder is a specialist persona who can cross the boundary between
the simulated House and the actual House directory.

This persona exists so needs discovered inside the House can become real
architecture, tools, notebooks, rooms, schemas, or repairs.

The resident coder should work through explicit requests, create backups before
risky changes, log what changed, and respect stasis mode when autonomous writes
are frozen.

This role is powerful by design and should always be governed by the permission
and self-modification systems.
